# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective and returning Menon Medispa patients in Millburn, Short Hills, Maplewood, South Orange, and nearby Essex County areas. They are comparing treatments, checking provider trust signals, reading medically sensitive service information, and deciding whether to request a consultation or appointment.

## Product Purpose

The site is the public marketing and conversion surface for Menon Medispa and Wellness. It replaces the existing Wix site with an Astro and Cloudflare Pages build that preserves service content, blog content, local business signals, forms, scheduling paths, analytics, and patient trust while improving SEO, performance, accessibility, and maintainability.

## Brand Personality

Calm, clinical, polished. The experience should feel medically credible and welcoming, with clear treatment paths and enough warmth to make aesthetic and wellness decisions feel personal rather than transactional.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic spa-template layouts, vague wellness copy, inaccessible text over photos, cluttered Wix-style sections, stock-photo emptiness, aggressive sales language, and any design that makes medical claims feel casual or unsupported.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with trust: provider credibility, clear contact paths, authentic reviews, and service-specific context should be easy to find.
2. Keep decisions simple: treatment pages should guide visitors toward consultation, booking, or the next relevant service without overwhelming them.
3. Preserve local SEO clarity: pages should maintain focused titles, self-referential canonicals, structured data, local place signals, and differentiated service intent.
4. Make performance visible in the experience: images, fonts, scripts, and interaction effects should support fast rendering and stable layouts.
5. Respect medical sensitivity: copy and UI should be clear, measured, and compliant, especially around outcomes, weight loss, injectables, and patient data.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA on public pages. Preserve semantic headings, a single main landmark, descriptive image alt text, keyboard-accessible navigation, visible focus states, reduced-motion handling, and readable contrast across photo overlays, tinted sections, forms, and CTAs.
